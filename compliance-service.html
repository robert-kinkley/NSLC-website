Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is CLIA compliance?

CLIA (Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments) is a federal law that regulates laboratory testing. CLIA compliance means your laboratory follows all requirements for quality, personnel, testing procedures, and documentation to ensure accurate and reliable patient results.

What does COLA compliance mean?

COLA is an accrediting organization approved by CMS. COLA compliance means your laboratory meets COLA’s accreditation standards, which focus on quality systems, policies, and best practices for moderate complexity labs.

Why does my moderate complexity laboratory need compliance support?

Compliance requirements are complex and constantly evolving. Our consulting services help your lab stay current with CLIA and COLA regulations, reduce risk of deficiencies, and maintain smooth operations while focusing on patient care.

Do you help with both CLIA and COLA compliance?

Yes. We provide comprehensive compliance support for both CLIA-regulated laboratories and COLA-accredited labs, including policy development, staff training, quality assurance, and inspection readiness.

Do you provide both CLIA and COLA inspection support?

What areas of compliance do you cover?

How do you support labs preparing for an inspection?

Do you provide staff training for compliance?
