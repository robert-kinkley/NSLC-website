Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What happens during a CLIA inspection?

A CLIA inspection evaluates your laboratory’s compliance with federal requirements. Inspectors review policies, personnel records, quality control documentation, proficiency testing, and overall laboratory practices to ensure accuracy and patient safety.

How can my lab prepare for a CLIA inspection?

Preparation includes reviewing documentation, updating policies, verifying staff competency, and ensuring quality assurance and quality control processes are in place. Our mock CLIA inspections and gap analysis help identify issues before the official inspection.

What does a COLA inspection involve?

A COLA inspection focuses on laboratory quality systems, staff training, and documentation. It ensures your lab meets COLA accreditation standards, including policies, procedures, quality management, and ongoing compliance practices.

Why should I use inspection support services?

Inspection support reduces compliance risks, saves staff time, and ensures your lab is fully prepared for CLIA or COLA surveys. We provide training, document review, and corrective action planning so your lab is inspection-ready year-round.

Do you provide both CLIA and COLA inspection support?

Yes. We offer tailored support for both CLIA inspections and COLA accreditation surveys, ensuring your moderate complexity laboratory meets all regulatory and accreditation requirements.

How often do CLIA and COLA inspections occur?

CLIA laboratories are typically inspected every two years, while COLA inspections occur every two years as part of the accreditation process. Staying prepared at all times ensures your lab avoids penalties and continues smooth operations.

How do I get started with your inspection support services?

Contact us to schedule a consultation. We’ll assess your laboratory’s readiness, conduct a mock inspection if needed, and create a customized plan to prepare your lab for a successful CLIA or COLA survey.
